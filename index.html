Designing for me is about creating clarity in complex environments. When someone turns to a product hoping to solve a problem, my goal is for them to immediately feel a sense of relief, understanding what to do, recognizing the value the product provides, and experiencing the satisfaction of using something that feels intuitive, thoughtful, and genuinely helpful.

My approach combines critical thinking, research, collaboration, intention and a certain level of boldness to create solutions that generate long-term value for both users and organizations. For this, flexibility is an essential part of my daily routine: whether in defining scope, navigating constraints, adjusting priorities quickly, managing stakeholders or continuously learning new skills and perspectives.

My leadership style is deeply grounded in active listening. For me, listening is the foundation from which collaboration, trust, and strong team dynamics are built. It allows me to better understand people, navigate different perspectives, and provide the clarity, direction, and accountability needed when making complex product decisions. Rather than positioning myself as someone who always has the answers, I focus on creating the conditions for teams to think critically, contribute confidently, and move forward together.

I also value transparency to create the kind of environment I would want for myself: one where people feel safe to express themselves, experiment, make mistakes with accountability, and continuously learn throughout the process.

Supporting people and driving outcomes are, for me, two sides of the same coin. I do this by helping teams cut through noise to focus on what truly matters, bridging gaps across disciplines, and anchoring decisions in both data and a clear sense of where we're headed.

Through my MSc research in Speculative Design and Futures Thinking, I've developed the belief that design should respond not only to the present, but also to possible futures. Across both my academic research and professional practice, I explore how emerging technologies such as GenAI influence human behavior, shape digital culture, and redefine the way people interact with systems, products, and machines.

This perspective strongly influences the way I approach design within the industry. By questioning assumptions and exploring alternative scenarios, I aim to help organizations anticipate change, identify risks, and build more responsible, inclusive, and future-conscious solutions. In a world increasingly shaped by rapid technological acceleration, I believe designers have an important role in shaping the conversations around the future we collectively want to create.

Matheus Petroni,
or just Mat

Lead Product Designer experienced in guiding teams across multiple initiatives and business units, focused on transforming complex problems into clear, intuitive, and scalable digital experiences that balance user needs with business goals. My background covers research, prototyping, usability, and product strategy, grounded in a strong visual and interface design foundation. I value active listening, collaboration, and creating environments where teams can grow and navigate complexity together.

Alongside industry work, I explore the intersection of design, emerging technologies, and human-computer interaction through academic research in Speculative Design and Futures Thinking. I hold a Bachelor's in Graphic Design from São Paulo State University (2017) and an MSc in Human-Computer Interaction and Artificial Intelligence from Pontifical Catholic University of São Paulo (2023).
